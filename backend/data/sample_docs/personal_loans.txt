PERSONAL LOANS - COMPLETE GUIDE
=====================================

WHAT IS A PERSONAL LOAN?
A personal loan is an unsecured loan provided by banks and financial institutions to individuals for personal use. Unlike home or car loans, personal loans do not require collateral. Borrowers receive a lump sum and repay it in fixed monthly installments (EMIs) over a defined tenure.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
- Age: 21 to 60 years (salaried), 25 to 65 years (self-employed)
- Minimum monthly income: INR 25,000 (salaried), INR 3 lakh annual (self-employed)
- Credit score: 700 or above (CIBIL)
- Employment: Minimum 2 years work experience; 1 year at current employer
- Nationality: Indian resident

INTEREST RATES
Personal loan interest rates typically range from 10.5% to 24% per annum depending on:
- Credit score (higher score = lower rate)
- Income and employment stability
- Loan amount and tenure
- Relationship with the bank (existing customers may get preferential rates)
- Market conditions and RBI repo rate

LOAN AMOUNTS AND TENURE
- Minimum loan amount: INR 50,000
- Maximum loan amount: INR 40 lakhs (subject to eligibility)
- Loan tenure: 12 months to 60 months (1 to 5 years)
- Processing time: 24 to 72 hours for approved applications

FEES AND CHARGES
- Processing fee: 1% to 3% of the loan amount (plus GST)
- Prepayment charges: Nil after 12 EMI payments; 2% before that
- Late payment penalty: 2% per month on overdue amount
- Documentation charges: INR 500 to INR 1,000
- Stamp duty: As per state regulations
- Cheque bounce charges: INR 500 per bounce

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
For Salaried Employees:
- PAN Card (mandatory)
- Aadhaar Card or any government-issued photo ID
- Last 3 months salary slips
- Last 6 months bank statements
- Latest Form 16 or ITR (Income Tax Return)
- Passport-sized photographs

For Self-Employed Individuals:
- PAN Card
- Aadhaar Card or photo ID
- Last 2 years ITR with computation
- Business registration certificate
- Last 12 months bank statements
- Profit & Loss account and Balance Sheet

TIPS FOR GETTING THE BEST RATE
1. Maintain a CIBIL score above 750
2. Keep your existing EMIs below 40% of monthly income (debt-to-income ratio)
3. Avoid multiple loan applications in a short period
4. Maintain a stable employment history
5. Existing customers with good repayment history get better rates
